Boss Exam

The boss is a final exam spread out on two days taken at MMI. It is called like that, because it is the last exam before the holidays. It's the exam that helps us know our strengths and weaknesses. I took three "Bosses" during my two years at college. It's a intense and stressful experience. Each student shows off what he knows best, if it's writing, design or development. The first day is dedicated to communication and to design. We have to write our texts and code our model in html / css. The second day is entirely devoted to development. This is where the project takes life ... Or not. Usually not.

My work

My web site presents Gore Verbinski, a movie director. I show a few of his films and a more complete video library. The second day, I've used VueJs. I have mainly used it to load content and manage navigation. For me, my last "Boss" went well. I felt inspired and respected time constraints. My model wasn't indeed, the most complex one, but the code was clean. And I used the exam to learn a new technology, Bootstrap. I wanted to challenge myself to learn something new and use it that same day while taking my exam. And it worked.
